Aburi, Ghana


CLIMATE CHANGE AND THE HOPE FOR THE FUTURE

What is climate change? In common usage, climate change describes global warming—the ongoing increase in global average temperature—and its effects on Earth’s climate system. Climate change in a broader sense also includes previous long-term changes to Earth’s climate. How climate change has impacted our lives There is no doubt that the impact of climate change in Ghana is beginning to manifest with evidence abounding that temperatures in all the ecological zones are rising, whereas rainfall levels have been generally reducing and patterns have increasingly becoming erratic. Unless mechanisms are carefully and systematically put in place to reduce vulnerability, climate change may pose serious challenges to national development The impacts of climate change include warming temperatures, changes in precipitation, increases in the frequency or intensity of some extreme weather events, and rising sea levels. These impacts threaten our health by affecting the food we eat, the water we drink, the air we breathe, and the weather we experience On employment, increase in temperature, decrease in rainfall and its unpredictability is likely to jeopardize the employment of about 60% of the active population, the majority of whom are small scale rural farmers, resulting in unsustainable livelihoods with negative consequences for poverty, health, education, gender equality environmental degradation and food security. Food security is expected to be affected in the sense that the reliance on rain-fed and low-input agriculture will mean that output will decline, resulting in lower incomes for farmers. This will in turn increase poverty levels and heighten the risk of malnutrition. In the transport sector, due to the changing weather patterns road networks easily get damaged. They get damaged as a result of either erosion or flooding or even extreme heat. This has resulted in lots of accidents on our roads damaging properties, causing deaths and leading many disabled. Personal Impact We often make the mistake in our everyday lives of thinking that climate change is a problem that doesn’t really affect us without realizing we are all suffering in our everyday lives from climate change either directly or indirectly. For some time now, it has been raining almost everyday and this really affects my studies as a student. Due to the frequent downpour of rain, movement becomes difficult either you’re going to work or to school. This mostly leads to most of my classes being cancelled or postponed, leaving us without any option than considering having our classes online which sometimes doesn’t come off because of network challenges, thereby slowing the teaching and learning process. Hope for the future Climate change may inevitably continue, however our actions now will determine the rate at which it occurs. Everyone deserves a healthy, safe environment and sustainable future. Through recycling, practicing afforestation, switching energy systems from fossil fuel to renewable like solar or wind to reduce the emissions driving the climate change etc…, we will gradually have the future we seek regarding to climate change
