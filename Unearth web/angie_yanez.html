The Legacy of Crystalia

There were 5 fairies in the distant mountain ranges that surrounded the town of Enherbola, each of which was in charge of one of the four elements of the nature. The first fairy was Evangelice, who was the fairy of the water second was Harmony, who was the earth fairy the third was Aria, who was in charge of the fire fairy the fourth was Nymphadora, who was in charge of the air fairy and the eldest fairy was Crystalia, who was the mother fairy who he could control all 4 elements, but he was of an advanced age. Things were not going well in the city of Enherbola, and the new king wanted to mark the beginning of a more modern era through the construction of buildings and shopping malls, but to do that he had to do a lot of deforestation. While not everyone in the city approved of these changes, they could not resist the rey. The fairies found out about this, but they did not agree because it was their duty to preserve nature and all its inhabitants. The fairy mother decided to take more drastic measures after realizing that that the king would not change his mind. He decided to send her storms, hurricanes and fires. As a result, the town experienced numerous changes in the climate, some beneficial and some detrimental. The people were running out of food and complaints reached the king, who decided to seek the help of the village magician, the king arrived with the magician and two soldiers, and it was not enough that the king was completely within the house of the magician when the magician was already spoken. The magician said in a low voice that the king could hear, "You come for the problems of the town, right?" The king was silent as he considered his answer. "Actually, he's right" All this is being done by the fairies, said the wizard. Because? For your crimes against nature, the magician smiled and motioned for the king to leave. out. The king had decided to cancel everything he was doing in the town after to reflect on the magician's advice. The fairies realized this and decided to stop everything, but at that moment the fairy mother was very exhausted. Unfortunately, the fairy mother passed away a few days later. the fairies were devastated, but then an owl arrived with a letter from the king thanking them for having taught him a valuable lesson: take care of nature, take care of its people and take care of its inhabitants. The fairies were moved to read the letter and felt gratitude towards the king for understanding the importance of preserving nature. despite the loss of the fairy mother, they found comfort in knowing that their sacrifice was not in vain and that they had managed to convey an important message to the king. The fairies continued to protect nature and guide the people of Enherbola and the people learned to live in balance with nature and between themselves thanks to the fairies.
